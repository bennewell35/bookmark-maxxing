# Recipe: LinkedIn Saves to Skills

Turn saved LinkedIn posts into reusable skills.

## Use This When

You save a lot of LinkedIn posts but rarely revisit them.

This is especially useful for:

- career development
- founder lessons
- GTM and sales
- product strategy
- management
- AI workflows
- engineering leadership

## Outcome

By the end, you should have:

- a source map
- 3-7 recurring themes
- skill cards for the strongest themes
- a study guide or article outline

## Step 1: Open Saved LinkedIn Posts

Open LinkedIn and go to saved items.

Stay read-only.

Do not like, comment, repost, connect, follow, message, or unsave anything.

## Step 2: Collect a Bounded Window

Choose one:

- this week
- last 25 saved posts
- one topic, such as AI, leadership, product, or sales

Do not review everything at once. Bookmark Maxxing works best in small slices.

## Step 3: Capture Source Items

For each item, capture:

```text
Author:
URL:
Date:
Post/title:
Linked artifact:
Why it matters:
Possible theme:
```

## Step 4: Filter Noise

Ignore:

- generic motivation
- personal updates with no reusable lesson
- job announcements unless they reveal a useful playbook
- vendor hype with no workflow
- content you saved only for social reasons

Keep:

- repeated operating principles
- practical checklists
- examples with clear steps
- posts that changed how you would work
- ideas you saved from people you want to study

## Step 5: Cluster Themes

Look for repetition.

Examples:

```text
Theme: hiring signal
Theme: founder distribution
Theme: customer discovery
Theme: product strategy
Theme: AI workflow design
Theme: leadership cadence
```

## Step 6: Convert Themes into Skills

Use this format:

```text
Theme:
Skill:
Trigger:
Source signals:
Workflow:
Proof gate:
Anti-pattern:
```

Example:

```text
Theme: customer discovery
Skill: Turn saved customer insight posts into a weekly interview review ritual.
Trigger: You have multiple saved posts about customer calls, support tickets, or feedback loops.
Workflow:
1. Collect the saved posts.
2. Extract repeated questions or review rhythms.
3. Build a weekly review checklist.
4. Apply it to one real customer conversation.
Proof gate: The checklist changes the next product decision.
Anti-pattern: Saving customer advice without changing how you review customer evidence.
```

## Step 7: Create the Study Guide

Turn the skills into a study guide:

```text
# Study Guide: [Topic]

## What I Saved

## Recurring Themes

## Skills to Practice

## People/Sources to Study

## Exercises

## Next Actions
```

## Step 8: Publish or Save

Choose one output:

- private study guide
- public article
- team memo
- skill cards
- checklist

## Copy-Paste Prompt

```text
I want to do Bookmark Maxxing on my LinkedIn saved posts.

Stay read-only. Do not like, comment, repost, connect, follow, message, unsave, or change anything.

Review this bounded set: [this week / last 25 saves / topic].

Capture each item as:
- author
- URL
- date if visible
- post/title
- linked artifact
- why it matters
- possible theme

Filter out generic motivation and personal updates unless they contain a reusable workflow.

Cluster the recurring themes.

For each strong theme, create:
- Theme
- Skill
- Trigger
- Source signals
- Workflow
- Proof gate
- Anti-pattern

Then create a beginner-friendly study guide from the strongest skills.
```
